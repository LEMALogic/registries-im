# Registries.im

The Isle of Man has been running internationally recognised registries for half a century.

Its ship registry is ranked first in the Paris Memorandum of Understanding annual report for compliance and professionalism. Its aircraft registry is a Red Ensign Group authority. Its companies registry underpins one of the world's most respected offshore financial jurisdictions.

The Isle of Man knows how to operate a trusted registry. And the Isle of Man holds EU adequacy status — meaning data registered here carries the same legal standing for EU-origin data transfers as data registered within the EU itself.

Data Asset Registers are what comes next.

## Why data needs a registry

Organisations have data assets they cannot currently prove they own, cannot adequately describe, and cannot reliably licence or share — because there is no independent, governed record of what they hold.

A Data Asset Register solves this. It is the equivalent of a land registry for data: an authoritative, governed record of what data assets exist, who holds them, what their provenance is, and what rights attach to them.

For a data registry to be commercially and legally useful, it needs to be:

- **Neutral** — operated by a jurisdiction with no competitive interest in the data it registers
- **Stable** — governed by a mature, well-established legal and regulatory framework
- **Recognised** — accepted by counterparties in the EU, the UK, and internationally
- **Competent** — operated by an authority with a demonstrable track record in registry administration

The Isle of Man meets all four criteria.

## The IoM registry advantage

- EU adequacy decision confirmed — personal data flows from the EU to IoM without additional safeguards
- Applied GDPR framework under the Data Protection Law 2018
- IoM Information Commissioner as independent supervisory authority
- Established Companies Registry, Ship Registry, and Aircraft Registry demonstrating administrative competence
- English law foundation with independent judiciary
- Political stability and well-established financial services regulatory environment
